Introduction

In our research, we've advanced in Generic Multiple Object Tracking (GMOT) by introducing the Refer-GMOT dataset and the Open-GMOT framework. Open-GMOT excels in tracking new objects and includes two innovations: CS-OD for identifying unique objects and MAC-SORT for combining motion and appearance data in tracking. Tested with the Refer-GMOT dataset and further evaluated on other datasets, our findings and resources will be made publicly available soon.

Methodology

Open-CSOD: To address these limitations of pre-trained VL models, our Open-CSOD comprises two modules as follows:

Module 1: Include-Exclude (IE) Strategy Using a pre-trained Vision-Language Model (VLM), we've refined object detection in our IE Strategy. It starts with parsing captions from "Refer-GMOT" into three prompts: general, include, and exclude, each defining object attributes. Then, these prompts are processed with a VLM to create and classify bounding boxes based on these criteria, enhancing tracking accuracy.

<attribute.object>  include  <attribute.part>  exclude  <attribute.part>

Module 2: Long-Short Memory (LSM) Mechanism: To mitigate False Positive (FP) arisingfrom challenges like pose, illumination, and occlusion, we propose a LSM mechanism.

MAC-SORT: We introduce MAC-SORT, as an enhancement of OC-SORT, which itself extends the SORT

The core idea of our MAC-SORT method is the effective balance between appearance cost and motion cost (IoU). This is crucial in GMOT, where objects often look very similar.

Unlike traditional methods like SORT, which rely on motion and struggle with occlusions or object disappearances, MAC-SORT incorporates advanced techniques for better accuracy. These include considering camera movement and detection confidence.

The innovation in MAC-SORT lies in its dynamic adjustment of the importance given to visual appearance versus motion cues. This is especially vital in GMOT scenarios with similar-looking objects. By fine-tuning this balance, MAC-SORT accurately tracks objects in complex environments where appearance alone is insufficient, resulting in a more robust and effective tracking system for highly similar objects.
